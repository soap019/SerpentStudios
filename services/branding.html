Build a brand for your startup

We're here to help you understand what a brand is, why it's important, and how you can build your own brand that sets you apart from your competition!

What is a brand, and why does my business need one?

For starters, it's much more than just a logo. A brand is the personality and voice of your startup; it's what other people perceive you as. There are a lot of things that influence this, such as your name, your colors, your logo, and even the way you talk! Having a brand is important for many different reason, the primary reason being that clearly communicates to others who you are and what you do.

Why branding is important
Communicates who you are

Branding defines who you are and what your mission is, helping you stand apart from your competition with a unique visual identity.

Creates team unity

A visual brand helps your small team come together with a unified mission. It's the figurative flag that your teammates raise and rally behind.

Builds customer loyalty

Your brand helps you resonate with customers that share your same values, building a strong connection that ensure they keep coming back.

How it all comes together

Creating the brand for your new startup seems like a daunting task, but that's why we're here to help. Your brand needs to represent you and your unique business, and a logo or color palette pulled off of google is usually not good enough, so instead we help you design your brand from scratch. Your brand should clearly communicate what your startup stands for and help others identify what you do and who you are at the blink of an eye. Below is our week to week process of how we help you create your dream brand.

Step 1: The Briefing

This is part one in the branding process. Before we can figure out what kind of logo you need or what colors best suite you, we first need to figure out who you are and what you’re all about. That’s what this step is for. We’ll start off strong with an introductory meeting discussing your mission, product or service, target audience, and more - all so we can both have a clear idea of why you do what you do, and how we can best help you communicate that to your customers.

Step 2: The Translation

This step is all about translating the words that describe your business into a visual identity that describest your business. Looking at a moodboard and a variety of images, we help you pick out exactly what you mean when you say "strong" or "clever"We go through a moodboard with you and help you figure out what it is that really speaks to your business or project, and what really speaks to the audience you’re trying to communicate to. This step is the key for our designers to translate your words into images, colors, fonts, and more.

Step 3: The Revisions

Now here’s the fun part! After we’ve figured out who you are and what that looks like, this is our presentation and revision week. This is the week where we present stylescapes, logos, and more based on the information we’ve already spent the past couple weeks discussing. This is the part where we show you fancy logos and fonts that help you blend in into your market, but stand out next to your competition.

Step 4: The Finalization

Now that we’ve figured out what works and doesn’t work for your company or project, we enter into the finalization phase. This phase is where we settle on what works for you and what doesn’t, or we make revisions to ensure that every asset, color and even word matches the exact message you’re trying to send to your customers.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Are branding and marketing the same thing?

    Branding and marketing are actual two sides of the same coin, both relating to how you are viewed by your customers and others around you. To put it simply, your marketing is what YOU say you are, and branding is what your CUSTOMERS say you are. If you think of it like a person - their "brand" would be the way they look, their clothes, the way they talk, and the way they make you feel when you're around them. The "marketing" for this person would be what they tell you they are. If they thought of themselves as funny, they would go around telling people how funny and silly they are.

  • Isn't a logo a brand?

    A brand is an identity system; it identifies who you are, and how your customers feel about you. A logo is a small piece of this identity.

  • Why are Brand Guides / Style Guides important?

    A brand style guide is important because it defines all the physical aspects of your brand so that they are universal. These standards can be passed around a team, handed down through a company, or used as a guideline for anyone developing digital or physical content for your brand. A brand guide defines exactly what colors to use and where, what to do and not do with your logo, how your brand should behave on social media and more.
